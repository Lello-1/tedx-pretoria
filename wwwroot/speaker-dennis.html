Dennis Ngango

Twitter Handle
@Dennis Ngango
Talk Title
Lessons I learnt from my Dad

Bio

My name is Dennis Ngango, I am a 20 year old South African dreamer, Industrial Engineering Student at the University of Pretoria, Humanitarian, Associate fellow for the Royal Commonwealth Society and a Global Youth Ambassador to South Africa at a World at School. John Green once said, what is the point of being alive if you don't at least try to do something remarkable? At the tender age of 16 I started working at a Community Radio Station and raised over 800 toys for a local charity. Now 4 years later, I've started my own Youth Empowerment website, Up & Coming Current and featured on radio and TV shows all over South Africa. I’ve been selected as the youngest TEDx Johannesburg fellow and represented the Youth of South Africa at the President Obama Town hall meeting in Soweto and this year’s June 16 Commemoration.

In addition, I’ve became a columnist for well-known publications in South Africa like News24 to say the least while still being an influential Community leader and academic achiever. In the beginning of the year, I was selected as a Global Youth Ambassador to South Africa at a World at School by UN Secretary Ban Ki Moon and former Prime Minister Gordon Brown. Together 500 young activist and I are pressuring world leaders to prioritize education through the #UpForSchool Petition which will be handed in during the UN General Assembly this year. I’ve received support in this regard from Desmond Tutu, Mmusi Maimane, Yvonne Chaka Chaka, Catherine Grenfell and many more. I’m recommending myself because I believe I am a dedicated, enthusiastic and conscientious person with a powerful empowering message to share!
